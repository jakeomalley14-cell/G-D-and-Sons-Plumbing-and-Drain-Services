Warning Signs to Watch For

  • → Multiple drains slow at the same time
  • → Gurgling sounds from toilets or sinks
  • → Sewer odors inside or outside
  • → Water backing up in tubs or basements
  • → Wet patches in your yard above the sewer line
  • → Frequent toilet clogs in different bathrooms

Drain & Sewer FAQs

My main drain is backed up — is that an emergency?

Yes. When the main sewer line backs up, every drain in the house stops working and you risk sewage backup into the lowest fixtures (usually the basement). Call Glen right away.

What is a house trap and why might I want it removed?

A house trap is an old-style fitting in the main drain line that was meant to block sewer gas. In modern systems they're not needed and they cause repeated clogs and backups. Glen can remove old underground or above-floor house traps and modernize the drain configuration.

Can you install a bathroom in my basement?

Yes — but you'll need a sewer ejector pump since basement fixtures sit below the main sewer line. Glen installs ejector pump systems that make below-grade bathrooms possible.

When should I replace my sewer line instead of repairing it?

If you've had multiple backups, root intrusions, or the line is ancient cast iron or clay, full replacement is usually more cost-effective than repeated repairs. Glen will give you straight answers based on what he sees.
