Why Licensed Gas Work Matters

Massachusetts requires licensing and permits for almost all gas work — and for good reason. An improperly installed gas line, a missing leak test, or an unpermitted line repair can lead to fires, carbon monoxide poisoning, insurance claim denials, and serious legal exposure.

Gas Services FAQs

Can my handyman install my new gas stove?

No. In Massachusetts, gas appliance hookups require a licensed plumber or gas fitter. An unlicensed install can void your homeowner's insurance and create safety hazards. Glen is licensed for this work.

My gas meter is locked out. What do I do?

Call Glen. Meter lock-out restoration requires a licensed plumber, proper repairs, permit paperwork, and a utility-recognized inspection. Glen handles the full process.

Do you install furnaces?

Yes, but Glen only services the furnaces he installs himself. If you need a new furnace, ask Glen about installation. If you have an existing unit installed by someone else, Glen can refer you to a furnace service tech.

What's an anti-tilt bracket and why does it matter?

It's a safety bracket required by code that prevents a gas range from tipping forward (for example, if a child climbs on an open oven door). Glen installs it on every gas stove install — not optional.
