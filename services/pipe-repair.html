Warning Signs

Signs Your Pipes Are Failing

Cast iron and old galvanized pipes give warning signs before they fail completely. Catch them early and you save thousands in water damage repair.

Sudden High Water Bill

A spike in your monthly bill usually means a hidden leak somewhere in your system.

Wet Spots, Stains, or Mold

Discolored or Rusty Water

Pipe Repair FAQs

How do I know if my cast iron pipes are failing?

Look for rusty water, slow drains, sewer odors, frequent backups, and visible rust or corrosion on exposed sections. Glen can do an honest visual assessment and tell you what's worth repairing versus what should be replaced.

When should I replace water lines instead of repairing them?

If you've had multiple leaks in the same year, your water is rusty, or your pipes are old galvanized steel, full replacement usually saves money long-term. Glen will give you straight answers — no upselling.

Do you handle stack line repairs?

Yes. Stack lines (the vertical drain pipes that run between floors) are critical infrastructure. When they fail, you get backups on multiple levels. Glen has experience repairing and replacing stacks in older Western Mass homes.

How long does a typical pipe repair take?

Most localized repairs are completed in a single visit. Larger water line or stack replacements get scheduled and quoted up front. Every job comes with a 90-day guarantee.
